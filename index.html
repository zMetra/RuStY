From Havertown to Headphones: Ru$tY Steps Forward with “Around”

Jack Lacianca, the 23‑year‑old rapper from Havertown, PA—just outside Philadelphia—leans into momentum with a new track featuring Corey Cash, adding to a growing catalog shaped in part by his time at the University of Pittsburgh.

By Culture Desk Staff

Published November 11, 2025

Ru$tY is part of a rising class of young artists translating small‑town urgency into big‑city ambition. Raised in Havertown, Pennsylvania, a few miles west of Philadelphia, he has been steadily refining a style that pairs melodic hooks with straight‑ahead delivery—music built for car speakers and crowded rooms alike.

His newest release, “Around” (featuring Corey Cash), positions him squarely in that lane. The record is paced and polished, emphasizing cadence and confidence over clutter. It captures a familiar pivot point: a young rapper moving from promise to presence, one release at a time.

At 23 years old, Ru$tY frames the work as part of a long game. After attending the University of Pittsburgh, he doubled down on writing and recording, building out a string of standalone tracks that hint at both range and intent.

A growing run of singles

Alongside “Around,” recent cuts include “Racked Out,” “Look Back Admire,” “Jigg To It,” and “Guns Extension.” The titles signal a through‑line: momentum, retrospection, movement, and reach. Taken together, they map where he’s been and where he’s aiming next.

While Ru$tY is still early in his catalog, the direction is clear—lean, focused songs that put voice and rhythm first. With “Around” as an entry point and a handful of follow‑ups already in rotation, he’s pursuing the work with the persistence it takes to turn local buzz into a broader footprint.
